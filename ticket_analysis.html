Key Insight

The Las Vegas Raiders lead all teams with a 339% average markup on the secondary market, translating to over $105M in potential revenue captured by resellers. This is driven by limited stadium capacity, tourism demand, and a passionate fanbase willing to pay premium prices.

Rivalry Effect

Historic rivalries command significant price premiums. The Packers-Bears rivalry, the oldest in the NFL, sees tickets priced 40% higher than comparable regular matchups. Cowboys-Eagles games in Dallas average 35% above baseline due to the intense NFC East competition.

Optimal Buy Window

Analysis shows the best time to purchase resale tickets is typically 10-14 days before the game, when prices dip 10-15% below the eventual game-day price. Prices spike sharply in the final 3 days as last-minute buyers enter the market.

Methodology & Data Sources

This analysis combines multiple data sources to estimate the revenue gap between primary (face value) and secondary (resale) ticket markets:

  • Face Values: Compiled from team websites and TicketIQ research
  • Resale Markups: Bookies.com analysis of secondary market pricing
  • Attendance Data: ESPN and Statista (~18M annual attendance)
  • Resale Volume: Estimated at 30% of tickets (sensitivity: 15-40%)
  • Game Type Premiums: Industry research on primetime and playoff pricing
  • Price Trajectories: Modeled from FinanceBuzz and SeatGeek research
